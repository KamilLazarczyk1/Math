Every exercise from Worksheets 6–10, solved step by step with the theory explained from scratch. Each result is independently verified and cross-checked against the official answer keys.

How to use this site

The material splits into two big themes. Worksheets 6–7 are second-order linear ODEs (the method of undetermined coefficients and variation of parameters). Worksheets 8–10 are multivariable calculus (partial derivatives, gradients, differentiability, extrema, the Jacobian, and multiple/surface integrals).

Inside each worksheet, every part shows: the problem, the theory you need (in plain language), a step-by-step solution with all formulas written out, and a check against the known answer.

About the formula sheets

You may bring the professor's Derivatives & Integrals sheet and the Laplace Transform tables to the exam, plus your own handwritten A4 sheet. Throughout the solutions, formulas that already appear on the official sheets are marked on official sheet, and formulas you would need to bring yourself are marked bring yourself. The Formula Reference collects them all, and the Cheat Sheet is a dense, printable list of everything you'd want on your own page.

Note on correctness. Every general solution, constant, derivative, eigenvalue and integral on this site was recomputed independently with a computer-algebra system and matched against the provided answer keys (Worksheets 6, 7, 10) or verified directly where no key was given (Worksheets 8, 9). Where the official answer is written in a different but equivalent form, that is pointed out explicitly.
